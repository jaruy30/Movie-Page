Big Hero 6

Big Hero 6 is an animated action-comedy film set in the futuristic city of San Fransokyo. The story follows a young robotics prodigy, Hiro Hamada, who forms a special bond with Baymax, an inflatable healthcare robot created by his late brother, Tadashi. After a tragic event, Hiro teams up with Baymax and a group of tech-savvy friends—Go Go Tomago, Wasabi, Honey Lemon, and Fred—to form a high-tech superhero team. Together, they uncover a sinister plot and set out to save the city, blending science, heart, and heroism in this thrilling adventure.

Finding Nemo

A clown fish named Marlin lives in the Great Barrier Reef and loses his son, Nemo, after he ventures into the open sea, despite his father's constant warnings about many of the ocean's dangers. Nemo is abducted by a boat and netted up and sent to a dentist's office in Sydney. While Marlin ventures off to try to retrieve Nemo, Marlin meets a fish named Dory, a blue tang suffering from short-term memory loss. The companions travel a great distance, encountering various dangerous sea creatures such as sharks, anglerfish, and jellyfish, in order to rescue Nemo from the dentist's office, which is situated by Sydney Harbour. While the two are searching the ocean far and wide, Nemo and the other sea animals in the dentist's fish tank plot a way to return to the sea to live their lives free again.

Howl's Moving Castle

A young woman named Sophie is cursed by the Witch of the Waste, turns into an old woman, and is unable to tell anyone of her plight. Unable to continue her job at her mother's hat shop, she goes to the ambulatory castle of the notorious wizard Howl and insinuates herself into his household. Sophie befriends Calcifer, the fire demon who powers the castle and who is bound to Howl by a contract, the terms of which Calcifer cannot reveal. They promise to help each other with their problems. Like Calcifer, Howl can also see through the Witch's spell, and he and Sophie fall in love. Sophie helps Howl confront his former teacher, and the Witch of the Waste.

Spirited Away

Annoyed about moving to a new home, 10-year-old scaredy-cat Chihiro finds herself stuck in the leather back seat of her parents' car while en route to an unknown neighbourhood. However, today, of all days, the girl's father is feeling adventurous. As a result, he takes a shortcut through a dense forest. And one fine summer day like no other, a strange, mystical wind pulls the family into a mournful town--an ethereal place balancing somewhere between reality and fantasy, the natural and the supernatural. Now, separated from her mum and dad, Chihiro has no choice but to pluck up the courage she never knew she had to find a way out. It sounds impossible, but for the first time in her life, Chihiro must confront her fears and the grotesque, menacing shadows hiding from the light. In this eerie spiritual journey to the mysterious wonderland, can life's frightening lessons help Chihiro find her identity?

Avengers: Endgame

Your Name

The story revolves around two teenagers, Mitsuha, a girl from a rural town, and Taki, a boy from bustling Tokyo, who mysteriously begin to swap bodies intermittently. As they live each other’s lives, they develop a deep connection, communicating through notes and messages. However, as they seek to meet each other in person, they uncover a startling truth about a looming disaster linked to a comet and an event that occurred years earlier. With time running out, Mitsuha and Taki must find a way to save her town and their connection before it’s too late. Your Name is a beautifully animated tale of love, destiny, and the threads that connect us across time and space.

Suzume

Suzume is an enchanting animated film directed by Makoto Shinkai. The story follows Suzume, a 17-year-old girl who stumbles upon a mysterious door in an abandoned building. When she opens it, she inadvertently unleashes a series of disasters across Japan. To prevent further chaos, Suzume teams up with a young man named Souta, who has been transformed into a small chair, to close these doors scattered throughout the country. As they journey together, they encounter strange and mystical forces, face their past traumas, and uncover a connection between Suzume's childhood and the unfolding events. Suzume is a visually stunning tale that explores themes of healing, memory, and the invisible bonds that tie us to the world and to each other.

Weathering with you

Weathering with You is a beautifully crafted animated film by Makoto Shinkai that blends romance, fantasy, and social commentary. The story follows Hodaka, a high school runaway who moves to Tokyo and struggles to find his place in the bustling city. His life changes when he meets Hina, a girl with the mysterious ability to control the weather. As Tokyo experiences constant rainfall, Hina's power to bring sunshine becomes a sought-after miracle. However, this gift comes with a heavy price, and as Hodaka and Hina grow closer, they must face the consequences of altering the natural order. Together, they confront the sacrifices that come with love and the choices that shape their future. Weathering with You is a visually stunning and emotionally resonant film that explores themes of youth, resilience, and the connection between humanity and nature.
